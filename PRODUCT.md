# Product

## Register

Cabin's website is the public marketing and writing home for Cabin. It is not the Cabin product itself. Its job is to make visitors feel the atmosphere of the future app, understand the philosophy, and follow the journey through essays and roadmap updates.

## Platform

Static web project hosted on GitHub Pages. The site must remain fast, crawlable, accessible, and useful without fabricating product screenshots, testimonials, waitlist numbers, or usage metrics.

## Audience

Primary visitors are writers and curious readers who may arrive from blog posts, project updates, social links, or the public GitHub organization. They may not be ready to download anything today, but they should leave remembering the mood and believing Cabin is worth following.

## Positioning

Cabin is a calm writing environment that grows with the writer.

It begins as a blank page. Chapters, scenes, characters, timelines, research, worldbuilding, plugins, exports, and AI assistance are introduced only when the work needs them. The site should communicate writing before features and atmosphere before explanation.

## Visitor Belief Ladder

1. This feels calm.
2. The website demonstrates Cabin's atmosphere before explaining anything.
3. This understands writers.
4. It names the frustration with existing writing software: too much complexity, rigid workflows, and software that gets in the way.
5. This is genuinely different.
6. Progressive complexity, local-first ownership, and AI as an editor rather than a writer make Cabin feel like a philosophy, not a feature list.
7. This is being built deliberately.
8. The philosophy, roadmap, and design process show a carefully planned long-term project.
9. I want to follow this journey.

## Primary Actions

Visitors should feel the vibe and become potentially excited to use Cabin when it comes out. They can also read the Blog to understand the approach. The Roadmap should prove this is not only a mood or idea, but something being carefully shaped toward a usable alpha.

## Brand Personality

Quiet. Deliberate. Literary.

Cabin should feel like entering a thoughtfully designed Scandinavian mountain writing room: fog outside, soft daylight, handmade paper, oak, linen, pine, generous silence, and carefully integrated tools.

## References

- Notepads website: immersive storytelling, confidence, full-screen composition, restrained typography, layered depth, crafted experience.
- Apple HIG and product pages: precision, whitespace, subtle motion, premium polish, typography hierarchy, disappearing interface.
- Obsidian: ownership, local-first confidence, respect for the user's files.
- Firewatch: quiet atmosphere, environmental storytelling, warm natural lighting, peaceful isolation.
- Journey: wonder, slow pacing, simplicity, deliberate empty space.
- Scandinavian interior architecture: oak, linen, paper, soft daylight, tactile materials, integrated furniture, architectural calm.
- Kinfolk and Cereal magazine: editorial whitespace, expressive typography, asymmetry, restraint.
- The Secret Life of Walter Mitty: quiet adventure, open landscapes, optimism, invitation to create something meaningful.

## Anti-References

Cabin must not feel like a generic SaaS landing page, productivity dashboard, enterprise software site, AI-first writing generator, cyberpunk interface, cottagecore decoration, sterile minimalist page, Apple copycat, Material Design demo, brutalist site, gaming site, or stock-photo mood board.

Do not invent screenshots, metrics, testimonials, awards, case studies, fake users, waitlist numbers, or finished product surfaces.

## Proof

The proof is the product vision, philosophy, public roadmap, brand identity, ongoing writing, GitHub presence, and active design process. The quality of the thinking and the atmosphere should carry the site until genuine prototypes, builds, and user feedback exist.

## Accessibility

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Every page must support keyboard navigation, visible focus, readable contrast, reduced motion, increased contrast, comfortable touch targets, semantic landmarks, generous reading widths, browser zoom to 200%, and minimal layout shift. Accessibility should feel like part of Cabin's calmness, not a separate feature.
