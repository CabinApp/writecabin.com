Why Cabin exists

Most writing software asks you to manage the tool before you can hear the sentence.

Cabin is built against that interruption. Structure is useful. Ownership matters. Assistance can help. But the writing must stay in the center of the room.

Manuscript

Writing comes first.

The manuscript remains central. Everything else is furniture around the work.

Structure

Complexity is invited, never imposed.

Panels arrive slowly, only when the story asks for structure.

Ownership

Your work stays yours.

Local folders, readable files and open exports keep ownership visible.

Quiet

Calm before clever.

Cabin should feel like a room prepared for thought, not a machine demanding management.

Editor

AI may question the work. It does not author it.

It can notice grammar, pacing and continuity. The voice stays human.

The project today

Cabin is currently in product design.

Its workflows, file model and interface are being defined before development begins.
