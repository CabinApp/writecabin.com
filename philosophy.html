Philosophy

The room is built around the writer.

Cabin is designed to begin with almost nothing and become substantial only when the writer's project needs more structure.

Start with one page. Let structure arrive slowly. Keep the files local. Let the software recede.

Progressive complexity

Cabin starts as a blank writing environment. Writers can add chapters, sections, acts, character cards, events, timelines, research and worldbuilding only when those things serve the work.

Writing comes first

No feature should make writing feel secondary. Planning, research and worldbuilding are supported, but the manuscript remains the center of the interface.

Local-first ownership

Projects live in local folders chosen by the writer. Files should be human-readable, portable and useful outside the app, with Markdown as the foundation.

Flexibility without chaos

Writers should resize and arrange their workspace, but Cabin should remain recognizable. Customization should support the work instead of becoming another project.

Calm before clever

Interactions should feel straightforward, quiet and deliberate. Even complex workflows should retain whitespace, soft surfaces and architectural calm.

AI as editor only

AI can notice grammar, pacing, plot and continuity concerns. It should not replace the writer, generate the work or pressure the writer into accepting its opinion.

Open exits

Cabin should export cleanly to formats such as Markdown, DOCX, PDF and EPUB. The writer owns the exported files without visible or hidden watermarks.

Modular growth

Plugins may add panels, commands, views, themes and small utilities. They extend Cabin without replacing the core writing experience.
